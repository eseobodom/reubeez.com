Fenugreek (also known as "methi") is a plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine, cooking, and even agriculture. It has small, square-shaped leaves with a strong, slightly bitter flavor.

In North Africa, Asia, and southern Europe, fenugreek was traditionally used for diabetes and to increase milk supply in women who were breastfeeding. It's also known for its many health benefits.

Today, fenugreek is promoted as a dietary supplement for diabetes, menstrual cramps, and other conditions and to stimulate milk production during breastfeeding.
This versatile plant, with its distinctive flavor and myriad of health benefits, offers a veritable cornucopia of opportunities for those seeking to enhance their wellbeing.
